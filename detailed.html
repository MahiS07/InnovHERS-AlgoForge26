The Grand Tour

Voyager 2 is the only spacecraft to have visited all four of the solar system's giant planets - Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Launched in 1977, it continues to transmit data from beyond the solar system, having entered interstellar space in 2018. The mission has revolutionized our understanding of the outer planets and their moons, discovering new moons, rings, and fascinating atmospheric phenomena.

The Voyager spacecraft

Interstellar Operations

Having traveled over 20 billion kilometers from Earth, Voyager 2 now operates in the interstellar medium, the region beyond the influence of our Sun. Despite its age and distance, the spacecraft continues to send back valuable data about this unexplored realm. Its radioisotope thermoelectric generators are expected to provide enough power to keep some instruments operating until at least 2025, making it one of humanity's longest-running space missions.
